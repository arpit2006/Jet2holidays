Sarhul

Jharkhand is a land of tribes. Different tribes are united by festivals and customs. Sarhul is an important festival of Munda, Oraon, Ho, and many other tribes. Sarhul literally means "worship of sal". It's celebrated every year at the start of spring when the trees blossoms and new growth begins.

Kolhaiya Mela is celebrated in the Hindu month of Maagh, particularly on the day of Basant Panchmi. The fair is patently held in Chatra District of the state of Jharkhand. It is a famous cattle fair which attracts natives and people from other states as well.

Baha

The Baha festival, i.e. the flower festival is celebrated by the Santhal tribe. Mohul, Palash, Simult Mango, Saal and many more beautiful colourful flowers mark the festival of Flowers. The festival extends for three days and is celebrated between February and March.

Tradional Products

Sohrai & Khovar Art

These ritualistic tribal paintings are created by women, celebrating harvests and nature, and are a unique cultural heritage of Jharkhand.

Tradional Spices

Jharkhand's tribal cuisine uses unique local herbs and a blend of spices like mustard, turmeric, and panch phoran, adding pungent and earthy aromas.

Traditional Foods

The heart of Jharkhand cuisine features staples like Dhuska (fried rice batter), Rugra (local mushrooms), and various pithas made from rice flour.

Dhokra (Dokra) Craft

This ancient lost-wax casting technique produces intricate brass figurines, showcasing the metallurgical artistry of Jharkhand's tribal communities.

Bamboo & Wood Crafts

With abundant forests, Jharkhand excels in bamboo crafts, creating functional baskets and decorative pieces, alongside traditional wood carvings.
