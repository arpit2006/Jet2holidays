Sarhul

April
harkhand is a land of tribes. Different tribes are united by festivals and customs. Sarhul is an important festival of Munda, Oraon, Ho, and many other tribes. Sarhul literally means "worship of sal". It's celebrated every year at the start of spring when the trees blossoms and new growth begins. The tradition is as old as 2000 years, from then to today, the ribals pray to the village God who protects the tribe. The Saal flowers are used for the worship of the deity during Sarhul. Sarhul signifies new beginnings, joy and togetherness. he worship of Sal tree symbolizes the worshipping of Mother Nature. All the prayers and offering are believed to go to Mother Nature that resides in tree. On the day of the estival, homes are decorated, a lot of delicious food is made, women wear white saree with red borders and perform Sarhul dance to mark the advent of New Year.

Kolhaiya Mela

Sun, May 25th 2022
Kolhaiya Mela is celebrated in the Hindu month of Maagh, particularly on the day of Basant Panchmi. The fair is patently held in Chatra District of the state of Jharkhand. It is a famous cattle fair which attracts natives and people from other states as well.The mela is said to have a religious significance too. Though principally a cattle fair in Chatrat like the Kundri Mela, the Kolhaiya mela too is held with a great zeal. People trade their cattles for money and also buy them for domestic uses. The fair has great pomp and show. There are stalls of food and jewelleries that are loved by the locals.

Baha

Sun, December 25th 2022
The Baha festival, i.e. the flower festival is celebrated by the Santhal tribe. Mohul, Palash, Simul, Mango, Saal and many more beautiful colourful flowers mark the festival of Flowers. The festival extends for three days and is celebrated between February and March. In the month of Falgun as per the Hindu calendar. The first day is known as the "Nayake Maha" (Priest Day), when the priest performs the rituals and purifies the houses and the diety.The second day is "Serva Maha", which witnesses the worship of flowers and accompanies song and dance. The third and last day is "Rasca Day" which is meant for total enjoyment. People eat together and dance in a community.The festival cherishes the importance of flowers which adds to the beauty of nature with colors and aromas. Flowers give the nature a sparkle that cannot be matched. Sohrai

Sun, December 25th 2022
Sohrai is a vibrant harvest festival celebrated by the tribal communities of Jharkhand, marking the end of the agricultural season. Observed after Diwali, it is dedicated to cattle and nature, symbolizing gratitude for a good harvest. Homes are decorated with beautiful Sohrai paintings, made using natural colors and depicting animals, plants, and daily life. The festival is filled with rituals, folk songs, dances, and community feasts, reflecting the rich cultural heritage of the region.
